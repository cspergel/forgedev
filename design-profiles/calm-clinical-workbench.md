# Calm Clinical Workbench

Design for trust, legibility, and reduced cognitive strain.

## Core Direction

- The UI should feel calm under pressure.
- Clinical or trust-sensitive work should never feel flashy.
- Dense information is acceptable, but the visual system must remain composed.

## Visual Tone

- Soft neutral backgrounds, restrained accent color.
- Emphasize legibility over brand theatrics.
- Use generous but not wasteful spacing in forms and detail views.
- Prefer muted dividers and subtle section backgrounds over heavy card borders.

## Typography

- Clear heading hierarchy with medium contrast secondary text.
- Body text should stay highly readable at normal zoom.
- Labels, helper text, and validation messages must be explicit and plainspoken.

## Forms And Review Workflows

- Multi-section forms should be broken into stable, named groups.
- Keep patient/case summary context visible while reviewing a form.
- Validation errors should point to exact fields and explain the correction needed.
- Use confirmation steps for irreversible transitions.

## Status And Risk

- Use color sparingly and consistently.
- Danger and warning states should stand out, but not dominate the full interface.
- Timeline and audit surfaces should feel chronological and trustworthy, not gamified.

## Avoid

- gradients
- hard-saturated accent colors
- novelty illustrations
- informal copy
- dashboard gimmicks that compete with case data
